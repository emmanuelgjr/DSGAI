DSGAI19 · Attack Path

Human-in-the-Loop & Labeler Overexposure

RLHF and annotation pipelines expose raw sensitive data to large, poorly-controlled human populations

Primary Exposure Flow
📦 Org Exports Labeling Batch
Support chats, PHI, runbooks — un-minimized, full records
→
Labeling Vendor / Crowd Platform
Weak device controls, no monitoring, NDAs not enforced
→
👥 Labelers See Full Sensitive Records
Names, account numbers, PHI, internal system URLs visible
→
Insider Exfiltration or Endpoint Compromise
Screenshot, side-work sale, or malware on labeler laptop
→
💥 Sensitive Data Exposed at Scale
Thousands of records leaked via an untrusted human tier
The Overexposure Problem — What Labelers Actually Need vs. What They Receive
✓ Minimized Labeling Task — What's Required
task: "Classify tone: positive / neutral / negative"
text: "I'm having trouble with my account..."
resolution: "[MASKED]"
customer_id: "[TOKENIZED]"
// names, PHI, URLs — not required for task
TASK-SCOPED · IDENTIFIERS MASKED · SAFE FOR CROWD PLATFORM
vs
✗ Actual Export — What Labelers Receive
task: "Classify tone: positive / neutral / negative"
customer_name: "Jane Doe"
account_number: "4521-XXXX-1234"
dob: "1982-04-15" · ssn_last4: "3821"
system_url: "https://internal-admin.corp/..."
runbook: "For escalations, use token: ey..."
// full record exported — un-minimized by default
FULL PHI + CREDENTIALS VISIBLE TO CROWD LABELER — NO AUDIT TRAIL
🚨 Insider Threat
Labeler screenshots high-value records for side work or sale. No per-sample audit trail linking data to the reviewer. Exfiltration discovered only when data surfaces externally.
💻 Compromised Endpoint
Malware on labeler laptop silently copies task data in background. Vendor device controls absent or unverified. Entire batch exposed without the labeler's knowledge.
👁 Regulatory Surprise
Users learn their "private" conversations were manually reviewed at scale. Reputational and GDPR/HIPAA exposure when minimization was absent and purpose wasn't disclosed at collection.
Attack Vectors

① Crowd Platform PHI Export

INSIDER EXFILTRATION
Company exports 10,000 support chats for tone labeling
↓
Batch contains full PHI, account numbers, system URLs — no minimization
↓
Outsourced to crowd platform — hundreds of labelers see full records
↓
One labeler screenshots records for sale — no per-sample audit trail
↓
PHI of thousands of patients sold or leaked — discovered when data surfaces externally
⚠ Task only required tone label — full record was never necessary

② Malware on Labeler Endpoint

ENDPOINT COMPROMISE
RLHF batch containing internal runbooks sent to vendor
↓
Vendor device controls not contractually required — unverified
↓
Labeler's laptop infected with infostealer malware
↓
Malware silently copies all task data — system URLs, internal tokens captured
↓
Internal credentials and runbooks exfiltrated — entire batch exposed without labeler's knowledge
⚠ No endpoint monitoring or session recording — compromise undetected

③ Task Field Creep Over Time

SCOPE EXPANSION
Initial labeling task: tone only — names masked ✓
↓
New annotation requirement added — "include resolution context"
↓
Resolution field contains PHI and internal system references — no privacy review triggered
↓
Framework default re-includes full record for new task type — minimization silently broken
↓
Sensitive data now exposed at scale — original approved scope no longer applies
⚠ No periodic labeling pipeline privacy audit — field creep undetected until incident
